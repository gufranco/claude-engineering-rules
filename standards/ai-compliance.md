# AI Compliance

## Disclaimer

This standard summarizes the obligations of EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689), Colorado AI Act (SB24-205), NYC Local Law 144, California AI Transparency Act (SB 942), California Generative AI Training Data Transparency Act (AB 2013), California ADMT regulations, US Executive Order 14110, Canada AIDA (proposed), Brazil PL 2338/2023, GDPR Art. 22, LGPD Art. 20. It is a technical default, not legal advice.

## EU AI Act Implementation Phases

| Phase | Date | Applies |
|-------|------|---------|
| Entry into force | 1 August 2024 | No obligations yet |
| Article 5 prohibitions + AI literacy | 2 February 2025 | Banned uses enforceable |
| GPAI + governance + notified bodies + confidentiality | 2 August 2025 | General-purpose AI obligations |
| High-risk most obligations | 2 August 2026 | Annex III systems |
| Article 6(1) + post-market monitoring | 2 August 2027 | Final transitional provisions |

Verified against [artificialintelligenceact.eu/implementation-timeline/](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/implementation-timeline/) on 2026-05-27.

## Risk Tier Classification

Every AI feature is classified before deployment:

### Prohibited (EU AI Act Art. 5)

Cannot be deployed in scope of EU AI Act:

- Subliminal manipulation causing significant harm
- Exploitation of vulnerabilities (age, disability, economic situation)
- Social scoring by public authorities
- Real-time remote biometric ID in public spaces (limited law-enforcement exceptions)
- Untargeted facial recognition database scraping
- Emotion recognition in workplace or education
- Predictive policing based on profiling alone

### High-risk (Annex III)

Triggers full conformity assessment + technical documentation + human oversight + accuracy + robustness + cybersecurity + logging + post-market monitoring:

- Biometric ID and categorization
- Critical infrastructure
- Education (admission, evaluation)
- Employment (recruitment, evaluation, termination)
- Essential private and public services (credit scoring, insurance, public benefits)
- Law enforcement
- Migration, asylum, border control
- Justice and democratic processes

### Limited-risk (transparency obligations)

Apply Art. 52 transparency:

- AI chatbots interacting with humans
- Deepfakes and AI-generated content
- Emotion recognition (non-prohibited contexts)
- Biometric categorization

### Minimal-risk

No specific obligations under AI Act, but cybersecurity + privacy + accessibility rules still apply.

## Transparency Notice Template

Required for every AI feature that interacts with users:

```
This service uses AI to [SPECIFIC FUNCTION].

What we do:
- [DATA INPUTS: e.g., "We process your text input to generate responses."]
- [DECISION TYPE: e.g., "We rank search results based on your past activity."]

What you can do:
- Request a human review of automated decisions.
- Disable [SPECIFIC AI FEATURE] in settings.
- View the decision log for your account.
- Contact us at [PATH] for questions.

This system is classified as [RISK TIER] under the EU AI Act.

Last updated: [DATE]. Version: [VERSION].
```

Localize per supported language.

## AI Disclosure Tag Pattern

Every model-produced user-facing output carries a visible label:

```tsx
function AiAnswer({ text }: Props) {
  return (
    <article aria-live="polite">
      <Badge>
        <span aria-label="Generated by AI">AI</span>
      </Badge>
      <p>{text}</p>
      <footer>
        <small>This response was generated by an AI model. <a href="/ai-disclosure">Learn more</a></small>
      </footer>
    </article>
  );
}
```

`aria-live="polite"` ensures the screen reader announces the AI-generated content. The badge is the visible indicator. The footer carries the disclosure link.

## Chatbot Self-Identification

The chatbot identifies its nature in its first message:

```tsx
function ChatBot() {
  return (
    <Chat>
      <Message role="assistant">
        Hello, I am the support assistant powered by AI. I can help with [TOPICS].
        If I cannot help, I will connect you to a human agent.
      </Message>
    </Chat>
  );
}
```

Never present the bot as human. Never use a human's first name only. If the user explicitly asks whether they are talking to a human, respond "No, I am an AI assistant."

## Deepfake Watermark

Generated audio, video, or images depicting real people include a permanent watermark:

- Visible watermark on images and video (corner badge "Generated content")
- Embedded metadata (C2PA Content Credentials or equivalent)
- Cryptographic signature when the producing party has a keypair (C2PA)

Per California SB 942 (2024): large GenAI providers must offer detection tools and embed disclosure metadata.

## Automated Decision-Making UI

GDPR Art. 22 + LGPD Art. 20 + Colorado AI Act: every decision with legal or significant effect provides:

1. **Pre-decision notice**: "This decision will be made by an automated system. You may request a human review."
2. **Decision explanation**: plain language describing the main factors and the outcome.
3. **Human review path**: one-click button to request manual review.
4. **Appeal mechanism**: documented process with timeline.
5. **Decision logging**: retained for the regulatory minimum.

```tsx
function CreditDecisionDisplay({ decision }: Props) {
  return (
    <article>
      <h2>Decision: {decision.outcome}</h2>
      <section>
        <h3>Main factors</h3>
        <ul>
          {decision.topFactors.map((f) => <li key={f.id}>{f.plainText}</li>)}
        </ul>
      </section>
      <section>
        <h3>What you can do</h3>
        <button onClick={requestHumanReview}>Request human review</button>
        <a href="/appeal-process">Learn how to appeal</a>
      </section>
    </article>
  );
}
```

## Bias Audit Cadence

Per NYC LL 144 + Colorado AI Act: annual bias audit for every automated decision system affecting users.

Disparate-impact testing across protected categories:

- Sex / gender
- Race / ethnicity
- Age (including under-18)
- Disability
- Religion
- National origin
- Sexual orientation
- Genetic information

Publish audit results when the system affects users. NYC LL 144 requires public posting on the employer's website.

## Training Data Transparency

Per California AB 2013 (2024): GenAI providers with $50M+ in annual revenue or significant California operations must disclose training data sources, characterizations, and quality measures.

Required disclosures:

- Source categories (public web, licensed datasets, user-generated content)
- Date range of training data
- Personal data inclusion (Y/N)
- Copyrighted material policy
- Synthetic data ratio

## EU AI Act GPAI Provider Compliance (from 2 Aug 2025)

General-purpose AI models with > 10^25 FLOP training compute trigger systemic risk obligations:

- Codes of practice (until harmonized standards available)
- Technical documentation per Annex XI
- Copyright policy
- Detailed training-data summary
- Information for downstream providers
- Designated EU representative
- Incident reporting to AI Office

## NYC Local Law 144 - AEDT Compliance

Automated Employment Decision Tools require:

1. **Annual bias audit** by an independent auditor
2. **Candidate notice** at least 10 business days before use: name of the tool, type of data analyzed, source
3. **Audit summary** publicly posted on the employer's website
4. **Alternative process** available to candidates who request it

## Forbidden Patterns

| Pattern | Reason |
|---------|--------|
| Rendering model output without a visible disclosure label | EU AI Act Art. 52 + California SB 942 + Brazil PL 2338 |
| Chatbot pretending to be human | EU AI Act Art. 52 |
| Automated decision with legal effect without human review path | GDPR Art. 22 + LGPD Art. 20 + Colorado AI Act |
| Emotion recognition in workplace or education | EU AI Act Art. 5 prohibition |
| Social scoring of natural persons | EU AI Act Art. 5 prohibition |
| Real-time biometric ID in public space without law enforcement exception | EU AI Act Art. 5 prohibition |
| Deploying high-risk AI in scope without conformity assessment | EU AI Act |
| Hiring algorithm without annual bias audit | NYC LL 144 |
| Consequential-decision algorithm without notice to affected users | Colorado AI Act |
| Training data scraped from non-public sources without lawful basis | GDPR + LGPD + AB 2013 |
| Generated content depicting real person without watermark | Deepfake disclosure |
